What this actually is

Most "top mutual funds" lists online just sort by trailing return — whichever fund happened to go up the most recently tops the list, which says more about which category was in fashion than which fund is actually well-run. MF Quant Analyzer instead scores every fund on 17 separate, independently-computed metrics — return, risk, consistency, behavior in different market regimes, and cost — then blends them into one score per fund, compared only against its own peer category (a small-cap fund is never compared against a liquid fund). The blend weights are not fixed by us: they live in the spreadsheet itself, and you can change them and watch every rank recalculate instantly.

The 5 cards

Returns
1Y / 3Y / 5Y CAGR, Alpha vs. category benchmark, Momentum, Average Rolling Return
Risk
Volatility, Max Drawdown, Recovery Score, Downside Frequency
Consistency
Outperformance %, Positive Return %, Rolling Stability, Persistence
Regime
Upside Capture, Downside Capture
Efficiency
Total Expense Ratio

What makes it different

  • No fabricated data. A metric that can't be computed for a fund (e.g. 5-year CAGR for a 3-year-old fund) stays blank, never filled in with a zero or a category average — the fund's score is computed from whatever metrics it does have, with weights automatically redistributed across them.
  • Fully transparent, fully adjustable. Every formula is a visible Excel formula, not a hidden model. Change any card or metric weight and every rank recalculates live, in the spreadsheet, on your own machine.
  • Peer-relative, not universe-relative. Funds are scored against the other funds in their own classification, so a steady debt fund is never unfairly compared against a volatile small-cap fund.
  • Free and self-hosted. No login, no account, no tracking, no subscription. Download the file and it's yours.

Who this is for

Built for someone who wants to look past this year's headline return and understand how a fund has actually behaved — how it holds up in a drawdown, how consistent its outperformance has been, what it costs. That includes funds with a shorter track record: this tool deliberately does not exclude a fund just because it's only 2-3 years old — a genuinely strong young fund is exactly the kind of opportunity a purely tenure-gated ranking (the kind most rating agencies use) would filter out before you ever saw it.

How it works

  1. An automated daily pipeline pulls official NAV data published by AMFI (Association of Mutual Funds in India) for the entire direct-growth fund universe.
  2. 17 metrics are computed per fund — returns, risk, consistency, market-regime behavior, and cost — each independently normalized within the fund's own peer classification.
  3. The 17 metrics roll up into 5 cards, and the 5 cards roll up into one Final Score per fund — using weights you can see and change yourself, live, in the workbook.
  4. Everything is republished daily: a live-refreshing Excel workbook, a static Excel snapshot for anyone without macros enabled, and the raw JSON behind both.

Frequently asked questions

Is this free?

Yes, entirely. No account, no login, no paid tier, no data collection. Download the workbook or the JSON and it's yours.

Is this investment advice?

No. This is an informational and educational quantitative ranking tool, not investment advice. See the full disclaimer below — read it before acting on anything here.

How is this different from a star rating (Value Research, Morningstar, CRISIL)?

Those systems typically require a minimum track record (often 3 years) before rating a fund at all, and don't expose their scoring weights for you to adjust. MF Quant Analyzer scores every fund on whatever history it has, redistributing weight across the metrics actually available, and every weight is visible and editable in the spreadsheet — nothing is a black box.

Why does a young fund sometimes rank above an established one?

Because this tool doesn't require a minimum track record to be ranked — a genuinely strong young fund is exactly the kind of opportunity it's built to surface, not filter out. That said, a shorter history means fewer data points behind some of the metrics (like Persistence or Rolling Stability), so it's worth checking the "History (Years)" and "Coverage %" columns in Ranking Summary alongside the rank, not just the rank in isolation.

What funds are covered?

Direct-growth plans across the Indian mutual fund universe — equity, debt, hybrid, and index funds — split into 16 peer classifications so funds are only ever compared against similar funds.

How often is it updated?

Daily, via an automated pipeline that pulls the latest AMFI NAV data and republishes both workbooks and the raw JSON.

Do I need Excel macros enabled?

Only for the Live workbook's one-click refresh button. The Snapshot workbook has identical sheets, formulas, and fully adjustable weights, but no macros — it works in any spreadsheet application, including on Mac and outside Excel entirely.

Is the source code available?

The data pipeline and scoring engine are maintained privately; the published data (daily JSON, both Excel workbooks, and this site) are public and free to use, under LessOneLabs on GitHub.

Is this affiliated with AMFI, SEBI, or any fund house?

No. This project has no affiliation with any Asset Management Company, SEBI, or AMFI. It uses AMFI's publicly published NAV data as an input, nothing more.
